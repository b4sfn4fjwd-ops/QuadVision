Any ball you throw follows this curve

Gravity pulls the ball down at a constant rate — that's where the −4.9t² comes from. The vertex of the parabola is the peak height.

h(t) = −4.9t² + 10t + 1

Bridge arches are parabolas

The arch shape spreads weight evenly — it's the strongest curve in nature and it follows a quadratic equation exactly.

y = −0.03(x−40)² + 48

Braking distance grows with speed² — not speed. That one v² is why speed limits save lives.

Every hill and valley on a roller coaster

Engineers calculate each parabola precisely — the vertex sets the peak height and the roots mark where the track levels out. Too steep and passengers black out; the math prevents that.
